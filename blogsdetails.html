Acute Appendicitis In Children: Laparoscopy vs. Open Surgery

Does acute appendicitis in children require surgery? What are the causes that could be responsible for this? Which treatment options are available? This blog aims to explain acute paediatric appendicitis in detail, along with the causes, treatments and recovery process required.

Worried about acute appendicitis symptoms?

A] What Is Acute Appendicitis in Children?

Acute paediatric appendicitis is a condition that is marked by the severe inflammation of the appendix in children. The inflammation here is a result of both blockage and bacterial infection. Appendicitis is most common during adolescence and the teenage years, with the highest incidence in children aged 10-19. Statistically, boys have a higher risk of developing this condition than girls.

B] What Causes Paediatric Appendicitis?

Swollen Lymphoid Tissue

Your appendix includes a higher amount of lymphoid tissue that helps strengthen your immune system. During infections such as colds or flu, this tissue becomes enlarged, blocking your appendix and preventing normal drainage.

A Hard Piece of Stool

Dry and hard stool can travel into the opening of your appendix and create a blockage. If not treated in time, the infection can further increase and even burst in certain cases.

A Twist in the Appendix

Although uncommon, the appendix can twist on itself, causing appendicitis. This cuts off blood supply, causing the tissues to swell and eventually become inflamed.

E] Recovery Process After Surgery

  • Diet: Clear liquids such as water, soups and rehydration powders are usually focused upon initially.
  • Light Activities: Walking can be introduced slowly. Activities involving rough play should be avoided.
  • Follow-Up: A follow-up after the first week is always recommended to ensure the child's recovery is proceeding smoothly.
