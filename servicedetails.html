Hypospadias Repair

Hypospadias is the second most common birth defect in children. It is a condition in which the urethral opening is not located at the tip of the penis. This results in the development of an opening that is somewhere towards the lower surface of the penis, perineum, or scrotum.

Hypospadias is known to be associated with other common defects like penile curvature (or chordee) and a hooded or incomplete skin. It affects the appearance of the foreskin and the overall direction of the urine stream.

Causes & Diagnosis

Hypospadias is a condition that is present at birth (congenital). As the penis develops in the male fetus, specific hormones are responsible for forming the foreskin and urethra. Hypospadias occurs when the hormones malfunction, leading to improper development.

With the help of physical examinations, a pediatrician can detect Hypospadias. The specialist, known as a pediatric urologist, will perform an in-depth evaluation. Surgery is usually carried out on infants in the age group of 6 months and 18 months.
